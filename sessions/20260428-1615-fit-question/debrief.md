# Debrief — Fit Question — 28/04/2026

## Questions posées

1. **Always Growing** — Tell me about a time you received very direct, critical feedback. How did you react and what did you do with it?
2. **Distributed Ownership** — Tell me about a moment where you had to make an important decision on a project completely on your own. What did you decide and how did you make that call?

---

## 🎯 Question 1 — Always Growing

**Exemple donné** : Over-engineering the onboarding feature at Chatain.ai. Co-founder gave feedback. Changed approach, feature shipped 2 weeks later.

### ✅ Points forts

- Example is real and specific — Chatain.ai, a concrete feature, a real co-founder
- Self-awareness: recognized the over-engineering pattern
- Changed behavior after feedback, feature shipped — there is an outcome
- Asking for the co-founder's input after feedback shows humility, not weakness

### ⚠️ Points faibles

- First reaction was defensive — fine to acknowledge, but the switch felt like compliance rather than conviction. What actually made you believe he was right?
- Outcome was weak: "some bugs but it shipped" — not an outcome. What happened after? Did users engage? Did it unblock the product?
- The "Always Growing" loop was incomplete: learned → applied, but never → "and since then, I do X differently every time"

### 🔍 Ce qu'un bon candidat aurait dit

- "Two weeks in, before the feedback, I was already sensing something was off — the plan kept growing. The feedback confirmed what I suspected." → self-driven growth, not just reacting to others
- "The next feature I built, I forced myself to ship a working version in 3 days, then extend. It took half the time." → closes the loop with a concrete behavioral change

## 🎯 Question 2 — Distributed Ownership

**Exemple donné** : Leading Open Source Together. Chose the tech stack based on team preferences (NestJS). Realized it was too complex. Made the solo call to scrap and rebuild with a simpler stack. Project is live.

### ✅ Points forts

- High-stakes decision — scrapping an entire stack mid-project is real ownership
- Managed two opposing camps and made the call anyway
- Project shipped and has real users — concrete, credible outcome
- "A fresh start for the team" — good reasoning, shows awareness of team dynamics beyond just tech

### ⚠️ Points faibles

- Too much "we" — "we chose", "we moved", "we rebuilt". This is your decision story. Every instance of "we decided" should be "I decided"
- Tech details were uncertain ("Express or maybe Next.js, I don't remember") — in an interview, being unsure about your own project's stack weakens credibility. Know your story cold
- No decision framework explained — how did you actually make the call? Did you set a deadline? Consult specific people? Alan wants the thinking process, not just the outcome

### 🔍 Ce qu'un bon candidat aurait dit

- "I gave myself one week to gather everyone's input. Then I made the call, and I owned it fully — if it was wrong, it was on me."
- "I decided" every single time, not "we decided"

## 🇬🇧 English


| What was said                       | Stronger version                                                                                   |
| ----------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| "I over-engineered a lot of things" | "I over-engineered the solution — I was optimizing for problems we didn't have yet"                |
| "I tried to defend my opinion"      | "My first instinct was to push back"                                                               |
| "We chose to rebuild entirely"      | "I made the call to scrap the stack entirely"                                                      |
| "Some bugs but it shipped"          | "It shipped two weeks later — a few minor bugs, but the core flow worked and users responded well" |


---

## 🔁 1 chose à travailler

**Stop saying "we" when talking about your own decisions.**

Before the interview, go through every example in your arsenal and replace every "on a décidé / we chose / we built" with "j'ai décidé / I chose / I built". This is the most important signal for Distributed Ownership — and you lose it every time you slip into the collective.
